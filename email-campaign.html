They Offered $7,000.
We Recovered $281,000.

A real case. A real fight. And what every injured person should understand before they talk to an insurance adjuster.

What Happened

A few months ago, our client was rear-ended in a serious collision. The medical bills came fast: emergency care, imaging, follow-up treatment — totaling over $29,000. The at-fault driver carried a $100,000 liability policy. State Farm, the insurer, knew exactly what they were doing when they opened with a settlement offer of $7,000.

That offer wasn't a mistake. It was a strategy. Insurance companies are trained to move quickly, make low offers, and close claims before injured people fully understand what they're entitled to — or how serious their injuries really are. They count on people being overwhelmed, trusting the process, and signing away their rights before they've had a chance to speak with an attorney.

"Insurance companies know which firms go to trial. We do. That changes every offer they make — from the first call to the final judgment."

We took this case to trial. Over three days, we built the evidence, selected the right jury, and told our client's story the way it deserved to be told. The jury returned a verdict of $102,000 — more than the policy limit. Eight months later, after post-verdict interest and attorneys' fees, State Farm paid a total of $281,000. What started as a $7,000 lowball became a result that actually reflected the impact this accident had on our client's life.

What This Means for You

If you or someone close to you has been seriously injured, the decisions made in the first few days matter. Do not give a recorded statement to an insurance adjuster. Do not accept an early offer without speaking to an attorney first. And do not assume that the system will work in your favor on its own.
