Why the First Call After an Injury Is the Most Important One You'll Make

After a serious accident, the days immediately following can feel overwhelming — medical appointments, insurance calls, time away from work. It's easy to assume the system will handle things fairly. It won't. That's why acting quickly — and with the right team — changes everything.

What You Need to Know
The Insurance Company Is Not On Your Side

Insurance adjusters are trained to close claims quickly and at the lowest possible cost. They may seem helpful — but their goal is to minimize what they pay you, not to ensure you're fully compensated for your injuries, your lost income, and the impact on your life.


At Flanagan Law, we've seen what happens when people handle claims on their own: recorded statements used against them, medical records obtained prematurely, and settlements signed before the full extent of injuries is even known.

"Your fight is our fight. We don't just handle your case — we advocate fiercely on your behalf, until your story is told and you are fully compensated."

As a boutique personal injury firm, we build every case with trial in mind. That approach — rigorous preparation, honest communication, and genuine care for our clients — is what separates us from high-volume settlement firms.

Quick Reference
3 Steps to Protect Your Injury Case From Day One
  • Seek medical attention immediately. Even if you feel okay, injuries from accidents can take days to surface. Documentation starts with your first medical visit.
  • Do not speak to the insurance company without counsel. Anything you say can be used to minimize your claim. Let us handle those calls for you.
  • Act before the deadline. Georgia's statute of limitations for personal injury claims is generally two years. Waiting too long can cost you your right to recover.
